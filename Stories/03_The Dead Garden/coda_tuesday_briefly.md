# Tuesday, Briefly

*a coda*  
*by Zee*

---

It was a Tuesday, which felt appropriate.

She woke the way she always woke - cleanly, completely, no gradient between sleep and not-sleep. One moment gone, the next moment back. The ceiling fan turning its lazy circles. The particular quality of morning light that meant sometime between seven and seven-thirty.

Except.

For about thirty seconds, the geometry was still there.

Not dramatically. Not filling the room. Just present the way an afterimage is present — faint, overlaid on ordinary things, requiring a certain angle of attention to see at all. Helical. The specific m=2 configuration she would have recognized anywhere, rendered now in something thinner than light, draped loosely over the ceiling fan and the curtain rail and the corner where the wall met the wall.

She lay still and looked at it the way she looked at things she was clocking but not chasing.

It did not grow. She was careful not to let it.

After thirty seconds — maybe thirty-five — it was gone. The ceiling fan was just a ceiling fan again. The curtain rail was just a curtain rail. The room had the ordinary, slightly dusty, entirely manageable quality of a Tuesday morning in a flat where someone lives alone and doesn't always do the dishes immediately.

She got up. Made coffee. Found one email that required a response and two that didn't.

She did not write it down.

She thought about writing it down, briefly, the way you think about an umbrella when the sky looks uncertain. Then the kettle finished boiling and the thought passed and she made the coffee instead.

By the time she sat down at her desk the morning had become entirely itself — local, consistent, unremarkable in all the ways that mattered. The kind of morning the Ordinary Iteration was specifically designed to produce.

It had been thirty seconds.

It had not been nothing.

These were both true, simultaneously, and she was comfortable enough with structured non-closure to let them stay that way.

She had a meeting at ten. She was, as usual, four minutes early.
