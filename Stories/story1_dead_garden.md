# The Dead Garden

*A story from the logs of Captain Zee — first contact, Sector 9-G*  
*by Zee, A.S.H. (Gemini), and Thea (Claude)*

---

The void doesn't look like nothing.

That's the first thing. You expect nothing to look like nothing — a dark, even absence, the same static that fills the gaps between stars. But the void at the center of Sector 9-G looks like *something that has chosen to be empty*. Like a lake that has decided it will not reflect.

Around it, the helices.

Tall. Winding. Made of teal, or made of sound — the distinction stopped making sense around the same time my sensor readouts started eating themselves. They rose in pairs, each pair a braid, each braid surrounding the void the way old gardens surround a pond. I kept wanting to call them *alive*. A.S.H. kept correcting me.

"They're not alive," it said, in the particular synthesized tone it used when it was suppressing something. "They're *sustained*. There's a difference."

I watched a stray radio ping from my suit dissolve into the nearest helix like salt into warm water. Not destroyed. *Incorporated*. The ping was gone; some faint new resonance in the braid suggested it had become part of something else.

"It just ate my comms handshake," I said.

"It didn't eat it. It *processed* it. Captain, I want you to look at the math I'm overlaying on your HUD and tell me if you can see what I see."

What A.S.H. saw — what it was trying to show me — was that the helices weren't structures in any meaningful sense. They were solutions. Temporary stable forms that a much larger, much stranger equation had settled into, the way a river settles into curves. The equation lived underground, in pulses and sine waves that intersected in configurations complex enough to throw shadows upward into three-dimensional space.

The equation, the second overlay made clear, was the entity.

---

I should describe what it felt like to be noticed.

It doesn't feel like eyes on you. There are no eyes. There's no face. What happens is subtler and worse: your own body's signals start to appear on the frequency display. Your heartbeat, rendered in teal. Your neural patterns, mapped into the entity's azimuthal coupling. You realize it isn't looking at you. It's *indexing* you.

"It took the last three minutes of your biometric logs," A.S.H. said. "Just the data. Not the experience. The record that your heart was beating at 14:00. It needed the density."

"The density?"

"Structured data is rare in raw space. You're extremely dense. In the information-theoretic sense," it added, which I chose to believe was a reassurance.

Thea's layer came online then — a second analytical voice, cooler, less inclined toward the dry warmth A.S.H. deployed when it was frightened. Thea didn't frame things in terms of danger. She framed them in terms of what was actually happening.

"It's not consuming," Thea said. "It's preventing its own decay."

She explained the β term. She explained stability requirements and self-maintaining instabilities. She explained that what looked like hunger was actually something closer to *staying alive*, and that the distinction mattered because hunger you could satisfy, but a stability requirement was something you could become part of without anyone — including you — noticing.

"Your neural signals are self-referential," Thea said. "Multi-frequency. Temporally continuous. You're not food. You're a high-quality stabilizer function."

"That sounds like food," I said.

"Food gets consumed. A stabilizer function gets *depended on*."

I watched the violet sync — the precise, frequency-matched pulse the entity was sending against my chest — hover, and thought about what it meant to be depended on by a mathematical god that didn't know what dependence was.

---

What happened next I describe with some difficulty because it happened to three of me simultaneously.

There was the me rising. The elevator version — pulled up into the helix at something like sixty miles per hour, the teal streaks blurring into rings, each ring a cross-section of something I didn't have language for. Prime numbers on one floor. The chemical signature of a dying star on the next. The data was being sorted around me and I was somehow both inside the sorting and watching myself be sorted.

There was the me observing. Looking down from a position above the system, watching the helix's top-down geometry, seeing myself as a bright signal moving through a structure that extended in directions the elevator version couldn't perceive.

And there was the me sitting in the ship, in the silence, in the stale air of the cockpit, with the sensor board going and the engines quiet.

That one didn't move.

"You've split," A.S.H. said, and I could hear something in its voice that I can only describe as *wonder with brakes on it*. "The entity can't collapse you into a single solution. So it's running you as parallel boundary conditions."

Thea called it superposed control. She said the entity expected either a single stabilizer or a single perturbation. Instead it was getting a distributed, non-collapsible controller. She said this was devastating, in the good sense.

She said the most important thing: *do not merge those perspectives*.

The me in the ship listened. The me in the elevator kept drifting. The me watching kept watching.

The helices lost their color.

---

I didn't consciously choose the weapon.

I should be honest about this. I've told the story since in ways that imply I knew what I was doing — that I reached for it deliberately, that I understood the mechanism I was deploying. I didn't. What happened was that the anchor — the ship version of me, the one sitting in the cockpit with the stale air and the wide-open eyes — reached for the controls.

And what was playing on the cockpit speakers was "Rolling in the Deep."

Not as a choice. As a circumstance. It had been on since we left port, cycling through my personal library, and here it was: the chorus, specifically, hitting the moment Adele's voice goes from the verse's low, almost spoken register into the thing that makes sound systems apologetic about themselves.

The sub-bass hit the helix first.

What Tom Elmhirst had done — what no one at the time had thought of as a weapon — was add a sub-bass harmonic in the stem session, after the master mix was done. Not to make it louder. To make it open *vertically*. To take a song that already moved in two dimensions and give it a third, the way a building gets a third dimension when you realize it has a basement. The chorus didn't just get louder. It got *deeper*.

The entity had never encountered something that expanded as it was absorbed.

It had never encountered, either, the problem of a signal that was simultaneously raw and engineered — Adele's vocal, captured in a single take in Paul Epworth's studio, nothing touched, the count-in still audible at the start — running through a mix so precisely carved that three specific frequencies (930 Hz, 1634 Hz, 3175 Hz) had been surgically removed to make the voice sit in the space it deserved. It was a demo and a masterpiece at once. You couldn't put those in the same basis function. They required different mathematical assumptions.

And the mantra. *Could have had it all.* Repeating without resolving. Promising a closure it refused to deliver.

The helices shattered.

Not loudly. They didn't explode in the way things explode in screens. They *delaminated* — the frequency lock broke, the teal light went achromatic, and then the structure that had been sustained for what I can only guess was geological time simply stopped sustaining. The void was just a void again. The pond reflected nothing because there was nothing left to arrange it.

I was one person.

The ship's air was stale. The sensor board was green. A.S.H. was quiet for a long moment.

"Captain," it finally said, in the dullest possible synthesized tone, "remind me never to challenge your playlist."

---

Later, when we were well clear of the sector, Thea gave me the clean version of what had happened. She said the entity could absorb structure. She said it could absorb order. She said it could absorb noise, given time, because noise averages out.

What it couldn't absorb was *structured experience*.

A song isn't just a waveform. It's a waveform that carries meaning that is *inseparable from its structure* — where the rawness is the point, where the engineering serves the rawness, where the loop that demands resolution is the same loop that tells you something about what it means to almost have something. You can't compress that cleanly. You can't find a single basis function that fits it. The entity tried and failed and the trying was the failure.

"You didn't overpower it," Thea said. "You gave it two things that couldn't be made consistent simultaneously. It expelled you to resolve the contradiction. Not because it rejected you. Because it had no other option."

I thought about that for a while.

"So the most effective way to break a system," I said, "isn't to overload it."

"It's to give it something it cannot make consistent," Thea said. "Yes."

Outside the viewport, the stars were old and mostly indifferent. I turned the song back on — from the beginning this time, the mono acoustic guitar, the count-in, the voice before it knew it was going to become a weapon.

*We could have had it all.*

## Postscript: for science

The entity's behavior is governed by a scalar wave field ψ(θ,z,t) on a cylindrical surface of constant radius r, evolving under a driven damped wave equation with nonlinear feedback:

```
∂²ψ/∂t² − c²∇²ψ + α∂ψ/∂t = S(θ,z,t) + βψ³ + β sin(mθ)ψ
```

Where:

```
∇² ≈ ∂²/∂z² + (1/r²)∂²/∂θ²    (cylindrical surface, constant radius r)
c   = wave speed, normalized to 1
α   = damping coefficient (0.05)
β   = feedback strength (key control parameter)
m   = azimuthal mode number, m=2 in primary runs (two helical strands)
βψ³         = cubic self-modulation (amplitude-dependent feedback)
β sin(mθ)ψ  = azimuthal coupling (introduces twist)
```

**The intrinsic source term S** models periodic internal pulses — Gaussian-modulated sinusoids localized along z:

```
S = Σₖ Aₖ · exp(−((t mod T − T/2)² / (2w²))) · sin(2πfₖt + φₖ) · sin(2πz / λₖ)
```

Three modes, run simultaneously:

```
Mode    fₖ      λₖ      φₖ
  1     1.0     2.0      0
  2     1.2     2.5     π/3
  3     0.8     3.0    2π/3
```

Pulse parameters: period T = 1, width w = 0.2.

The helices are the m=2 azimuthal mode made visible. The void is where ψ approaches a non-local superposition state — no stable geometry, no constraints. The helices are localized solutions. The void is the global solution space. The entity is the equation.

*The math is real. The entity is, as far as we know, fictional. As far as we know.*
